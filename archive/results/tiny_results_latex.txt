
To further validate the efficacy of GRA-CNN on larger-scale datasets, we conducted a comparative experiment on Tiny-ImageNet using ResNet-18 with a 50\% pruning ratio. Starting from a baseline accuracy of 61.10\%, the L1-norm pruned model achieved an accuracy of 58.99\% after fine-tuning. In contrast, GRA-CNN achieved 59.50\%, outperforming the magnitude-based baseline by 0.51\%. Both methods reduced FLOPs by approximately 45.5\%, but GRA's superior accuracy retention highlights its ability to identify and preserve semantically critical channels even in complex classification tasks with higher spatial resolution.
